Abstract:  Technology  is  an  essential  component  of  the  civilization  of  twenty-first-century  society  and  one  of  the  key  forces 
influencing  public  sector  organizations.    Public  infrastructures  like  transportation  systems  and  important  service  delivery  are 
managed using technology and powerful computing software. Artificial intelligence (AI) on the other hand helps to add intelligence 
to computing devices and applications through learning, reasoning, and perceiving things from data. However, the level of adoption 
or application of these technologies in the Nigerian judiciary seems to be low as seen during the covid-19 outbreak. The closed 
down of courts led to an increase in the backlog of cases due to the suspension of court proceedings. Even the option of filling cases 
using technology like WhatsApp could not function effectively. AI can This paper identifies some areas technology and AI can 
improve the performance of Nigeria’s judiciary and increase the speed of justice dispensation. 

Keywords: Artificial Intelligence in Judiciary, Information Technology in Judiciary, Artificial Intelligence and Legal System, 
Legal System in COVID-19   

I. Introduction 

Technology  is  the  binding thread  for  the  civilization  of  the  twenty-first  century,  and it  is  transforming the  planet in  all  spheres 
(Contini, 2020). Today, one of the key forces influencing public sector organizations is emerging technologies. These technologies 
are used to handle public infrastructures and service delivery.  The drive from manual systems to online service delivery is a key 
agenda of many countries around the globe and was the saving option for many developed worlds during the covid-9 lockdown. 
The use of technology was also essential in aiding some developed nations to even continue court proceeding online (Sourdin et 
al., 2018)   

Artificial intelligence on the other hand has also significantly transformed the world of computing by enabling devices to learn, 
reason, and perceive things from data and their environment (Xu, 2022). This level of intelligence of machines is made possible by 
machine learning algorithms that provide intelligent analysis of massive data (Contini, 2020).  Artificial intelligence (AI) has already 
begun to have a significant impact on the field of law, and it is likely to continue to do so in the future (Journal & Sourdin, 2018). 
Judicial systems are using Artificial Intelligence (AI) to analyse large amounts of legal data to help lawyers identify precedents in 
case law. The gains of AI also support judges with predictions on issues including sentence duration and recidivism scores.  

Apart software systems, AI boots and robots are beginning to transform legal practice in the form lawyers. A good instance is Robot 
Ross by Watson, IBM's cognitive computer. The robot demonstrated capacity to speak and interact with human language and to act 
as an intelligent and efficient consultant in bankruptcy cases.  According (Lu & Hu, 2019),    a robot called DoNotPay developed 
in UK, can help users in traffic related cases and to prepare necessary papers to defend their case. Another case is a legal robot 
developed in Israel which went in contest with 20 experience lawyers in business contract related cases. The robot was claimed to 
outperformed them with accuracy level beyond 90  (Lu & Hu, 2019). These adventures of AI in law gives prospect of what AI can 
do even Nigeria. 

However, the impact of technology and AI was not much felt during COVID-19 in Nigeria. The shutting down of courts stalled 
trials and reduced the rate of case disposal. The judicial system being heavily dependent on physical convergence of litigants, their 
lawyers and judge was adversely affected as a result of a halt in physical gathering (Olugasa & Davies, 2022). This setback was a 
lack of necessary ICT structures and software to drive a judicial system in a digital area.  As the world gets into a new area of post 
covid-19,  there  is  a  need  for  innovative  use  of  technology  and  Artificial  Intelligence  (AI)  to  drive  judicial  sector  to  achieve 
efficiency.  It will speed up decision making process for the judges and increase case disposal rate

II. Impact of Covid-19 Pandemic on Judiciary   

 The COVID-19 pandemic had a major impact on Nigeria's judicial system, which changed how courts function. Yet, the epidemic 
also offered chances for the adoption of cutting-edge procedures and technologies that could eventually boost the effectiveness and 
accessibility of the legal system. 

One significant effect is the delay in dispute resolution caused by the closing of courts to stop the spread of COVID-19. papers 
reported a surge in certain type of cases like contract  (Ahmed, 2021; Arinze-Umobi & Okonkwo, 2021; Ojienda & Adude, 2021; 
Prescott et al., 2017) and domestic violence (Ahmed, 2021). This led to increase in backlog of cases and suffering for those with 
urgent cases. 

Despite efforts to conduct court processes online, there were difficulties in cross-examining witnesses and determining the veracity 
of  evidence  virtually  (Olugasa  &  Davies,  2022). The  current level  of  technology  and necessary  software  that  can  fast rack  this 
development are not adopted yet, affecting the effective use of technology in legal system.  The level of connectivity in the country 
also played a significant role in access to justice, many Nigerians were unable to access to justice due to lack of technology  or 
internet connectivity(Olugasa & Davies, 2022). 

III. Smart Courts for Post Covid-19 Courts in Nigeria 

Smart courts will require the use of intelligent software as a means of filling cases electronically and will also involve use of AI to 
support systems for decision-making both for judges and the lawyer. Large volume of files and documents are file in many cases, 
requiring a judge to read and study much before passing judgments (Reiling, 2020). This reason alone can be the cause to delay in 
many cases. 

Limited use of online technology also greatly impacted the progress of trials during the outbreak of Covid-19 in Nigeria. Though 
technologies like video conference such as zoom, Skype were used in trying remote proceedings (Olugasa & Davies, 2022), they 
were  grossly  inadequate  for  various  areas  of  court  processes.  They  lack  capacity  for  file  documents  and  other  essential  cases 
management  systems  that  will  enhance  proper  documentation.   Some  areas  that  software  technologies  can    judiciary  are  listed 
below. 

3.1 Case Management systems 

ICT  can help  courts manage record more  effectively  by  automating  procedures  such  as  case  scheduling and hearings, case  file 
management,  and  case  progress  tracking  (Prescott,  2017,  Keong,  2017).  CMS  are  also  useful  for  creating  reports.  Several 
jurisdictions, such as the United States, use Odyssey  by Tyler Technologies, JIMS by Journal Technologies, CMS by Thomson 
Reuters, and Court Explorer to speed up electronic filing, jury selection, and other administrative procedures. Similar technologies, 
such as JUSTIS and e-Curia, are utilized in courts in India, China, and Europe. 

Sometimes, cases are never called or mention for years due to manual scheduling and human error.  Therefore, case management 
is a crucial step in the administration of justice (Ojienda & Adude, 2021; Sangwan & Navet, 2021) and  useful for assessing the 
speed and progress of cases and may even prevent cases from going uncalled for a long period of time.   

3.2 Electronic filing Systems  

Electronic filing systems, which allow parties to electronically file legal papers and court forms, are becoming increasingly prevalent 
in the judiciary (Emezue, 2020; Fenwick & Brownstone, 2002). It eliminates the need for paper filing and saves time. During Covid-
19, the Chinese Judgments Online, China Legal Aid Online, and other related platforms were used to file new cases and make court 
pronouncements available online even before the pandemic. PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) and CM/ECF 
(Case Management/Electronic Case Files) are also popular e-filing systems in the United States.  

The Chief Justice of Nigeria and other state chief judges instructed Nigerian courts to file cases using WhatsApp, email, or Dropbox 
during the pandemic (Olugasa & Davies, 2022). This approach of case filling is out of step with modern technologies. Though a 
platform for standard case filling has been implemented in a few states in Nigeria, including Ogun, Rivers, and FCT (Olugasa & 
Davies, 2022), however, in state without portals to file cases, the correct files cannot be posted in the method of practice guidance 
for the judiciary.  

 Implementation of proper e-filling system is crucial in all state in Nigeria to forestall issues of missing cases or people being deny 
justice because they are unable file case within stipulated time by law. It also allows people to file cases without leaving the houses 
and reduce the risk of travelling.

3.3 Remote Hearing 

In  the  wake  of  COVID-19,  videoconferencing  technology  gained  popularity  among  various  organizations. It  became  a  tool  for 
judges in many countries like UK, USA, India and China to hold hearings and trials remotely (Keong, 2017). It eliminates the need 
for in-person appearances by connecting courts with parties, witnesses, and legal professionals.   

Webex, and Microsoft Teams are three of the more popular video conferencing programs used in some countries including Nigeria 
(Olugasa  &  Davies,  2022),  however,  there  is  need  for  standard  organizational-based  software  which  Nigeria  government  will 
develop and continue to upgrade its functionality to suite its judiciary.   

3.4 Digital Evidence management 

 Nigeria judiciary do not at the presents seem to have technology to handle digital evidence, however, digital evidence is becoming 
more important in the court system as a result of technology's continued advancement (Kessler, 2010). Effective management and 
storage are required as they gradually enter court cases. By offering tools for storing, indexing, and searching electronic documents 
and  other types  of  digital  evidence,  ICT  can  assist  courts  in  managing  electronic  evidence  more  efficiently.  Some  jurisdictions 
already employ programs like Trial Director and Sanction, Presentation Assistant, and Prezi for this reason.  

3.5 Online Dispute Resolution: 

Nigeria government have some dispute resolution departments in some agencies, however, the need for in-person appearance made 
them ineffective during the lockdown. Online dispute management and resolutions are very vital in the current era of technology 
and post Covid-19 Nigeria  (Arinze-Umobi & Okonkwo, 2021; Zeleznikow, 2017). Though there is a concept of mobile court in 
Nigeria, it is completely a physical court which can change its location of sitting. 

As the number cases are growing as a result of human population, some jurisdictions are testing online dispute resolution (ODR) 
systems, which leverage ICT to facilitate conflict settlement without necessity of in-person court appearances (Rule, 2020; Mania, 
2015).  ODR  has  developed  as  a  viable  alternative  to  regular  judicial proceedings.  Prior  to  COVID  -19  era,  China  initiated  the 
development of online courts in 2015 to trial specific sorts of cases, these efforts were especially important during COVID-19 when 
physical appearance could not possible.  

3.6 Digital Transcription Tools: 

 For  proper  archiving  and  analysis,  digital  transcription  tools  are  used  to  convert  audio  recordings  of  court  proceedings.  This 
software enables the legal team to concentrate on other crucial tasks and aids in distant proceedings. One crucial aspect of need in 
Nigeria is to develop digital transcription tools for the local languages, this tools will be essential to transcribe witnesses testimony 
in local languages. 

IV. Artificial Intelligence (Ai) In Judicial System  

The major input of AI in courts is to assist judges in decision making and to reduce a surge in the number of cases that many come 
to  court    (Sanctis,  2021;  Wang,  2020).  Several  application  of  AI  in  court  processes  has  shown  remarkable  success  in  legal 
practice(Re & Solow-niederman, 2019). AI has found its way into judiciary system (Giuffrida et al., 2018; Godwin, 2021; Journal 
&  Sourdin,  2018;  Kioko  et  al.,  2022;  Xu,  2022),and  has  transformed  justice  system  in  the  form  of  an  “assistant”  judge  which 
complements the efforts of judges to improve accuracy of judgements (Xu, 2022). 

Some may believe that algorithms do not possess the capacity for fairness and proper interpretation of law or explanation of decision 
made (Soukupová, 2021), however, AI  can be thought of as an implementation of codified justice system that could bring about 
fairness and reduction of arbitrariness in justice system (Re & Solow-niederman, 2019). It has capacity to transform legal system 
by  predicting the outcomes of court decisions based on past judgements (Kauffman & Soares, 2020).  With Artificial Intelligence, 
possibility of efficiency in judicial system with high level of predictability and accuracy of case outcome exist (Mccarthy, 2020). It 
will also improve the quality of decisions and reduce time taken during trials and proceedings. There are simple and complex cases 
which into which AI can support judge in different front Like examination of evidence during proceeding and the generation of 
elemental adjudication instruments in a short period(Kauffman & Soares, 2020; Xu, 2022).  

4.1 Specific areas of AI application in judiciary  

4.1.1 Predicting outcome of a case 

Based on past data, AI can assist lawyers in predicting the result of legal disputes. This can enable them to decide more intelligently 
whether to settle a lawsuit or proceed to trial. Prediction capacity of AI application has been prevalent in many areas  of science 
before now and has been of great power for helping in strategic making decisions, however, it’s starting to be implemented in areas 
that do not seem to follow precise rule.

AI software developed in Texas University using US Supreme Court judgment from 1791 to 2015 was able to predict Supreme 
Court judges’ votes and decision with over 70% accuracy. This level of accuracy is said to be higher than the average human 66% 
predictive accuracy of jurists (Xu, 2022). Due to frequency of pattern of certain cases, a relatively large proportion of routine cases 
have  a  predictable  outcome.  In  those  cases,  the  court  rulings  are  relevant  for  input  data  into  AI-based  application  and  future 
predictions judgment of other cases (Mccarthy, 2020; Reiling, 2020). Users of such AI software can easily predict the chances of 
wining a particular case even before filling it and make right decision.   

4.1.2 Evidence Analysis  

One of the biggest challenges of digital era is the possibility  of digital manipulation and faking of evidence. A number of deep 
learning  and  AI applications  exist  to handle  the  complexity  and  evidence  management  (Wang, 2020).   The  Shanghai  judiciary 
developed  the  Intelligent  Auxiliary  Case  Handling  System  for  Criminal  Cases.  The  software  manages  case  through  Optical 
Character Recognition (OCR) and Natural Language Processing (NLP). The software is developed to map evidence to appropriate 
crimes and to review evidence to find probable contradictions. Variations and relevance of the evidence to the case can even be 
establish using the software. In the era of remote court proceeding, AI stands as an important component which will help judges to 
determine genuineness of evidence. 

4.1.3 Extracting Patterns in Case Files 

Pattern recognition is one powerful area that AI has thrived.  Machine learning algorithms are employed to recognize patterns in a 
large volume of data (Karatzinis & Boutalis, 2021).  Sorting several cases into categories or finding pattern in a case with large 
volume of information could be a daunting task; however, AI application like  ‘eDiscovery’ being used in American courts  performs 
intelligent analysis of case files before the start of a court procedure (Reiling, 2020). eDiscovery uses machine learning to extract 
the relevant parts from a large amount of information (Kauffman & Soares, 2020). This help to focus on the case more precisely.  

‘SOCRATES’  is a similar AI based application which was trained from over 300,000 previous court judgments  (Sanctis, 2021).  
The AI software scan and read through all the case file and cluster them so that they can be judged in blocks. It functions to screen 
out cases that are outside the jurisdiction of the court so that the court time won’t be wasted. Cases similar to previously  judged 
cases are return to the lower courts so that the previous judgments of the court on such cases can be apply on them. 

4.1.4 Sentence preparation  

Preparing court sentence can be challenging and time consuming due to large document to read, however, AI can speed up this 
process. A good example is VICTOR, an AI software used by Brazilian Supreme Court Which read all appeal cases filed at the 
Supreme Court and identify which ones are linked to certain repercussion. The software was claimed to have speed up the work of 
the judges. It is also being proposed as a support system to judges in the lower courts to reduce number of cases that gets to appeal 
court (Sanctis, 2021). With this very Machine learning application, the number of possible cases will be reduced with increase in 
processing of appeal. The software has resolved about 10,000 extraordinary appeals that should have reach the supreme (Sanctis, 
2021) 

4.1.5 Sentiment Analysis and Natural Language Processing 

AI can also be used to analyze legal texts to identify relevant legal principles, arguments or sentiment of a particular individual. 
This can help to establish motive or intent in a case. The judges will also have more informed decisions from such analysis. A close 
case  of this research in UK by European Court of Human Rights detects human right infringement base on choices and language  
usage (Mccarthy, 2020). The success rate accomplished by the software is close to 80%.  With this remarkable success, it can be a 
good support system for judges handling human right cases. 

4.1.6 Legal counsel  

 Legal advice helps litigants to take decision of on resolution of cases and reduce the need to court cases.  A number of AI software 
are being developed to help the public in such instances so that if proper decision and resolution occur, there won’t be need to file 
for  court  case.    Examples  are  AI  applications  developed  to  help  the  District  Court  of  East  Brabant  in  the  Netherlands  in 
administrative handling of traffic violation cases.  A similar software developed for advising parties on cases relating to housing 
dispute and accidents from collisions is being used by Civil Resolution Tribunal (CRT)  Columbia, Canada  (Reiling, 2020). The 
software offers legal advice and information to the public on paths to resolve dispute. This software are very vital in resolving trivial 
cases.

4.1.7 Legal research 

Legal research tools allow legal professionals to search and access legal resources. By combining machine learning and natural 
language processing, AI-powered software can spot patterns and insights in vast amounts of legal data that would be challenging 
for humans to discover on their own.   

V. Ethical Concern Over Use of Ai in Court 

Though the use of AI in legal system has prospect in empowering judiciary for speedy dispensation of justice; however, there are 
concern on the use of AI in court on challenges of infringement of peoples right due to result of data analysis  (Moroianu et al., 
2019). A number of issues were outlined by (Reiling, 2020) which include fairness of AI software to all parties during trials, respect 
to fundamental human right of all parties and non-discrimination. The problem of taking responsivity for whatever decision an AI 
system may take is also a very crucial aspect of law, an AI system cannot be sued for damages if it offers wrong advice or the 
service result damages.    

VI. Conclusion  

As populations grow, crisis and litigations also grow among humans. This fact has led to an  ever-increasing surge of cases that 
come before judicial officers for adjudication. However, Judiciary in Nigeria and globally are challenged daily with large number 
of  cases  beyond  the available manpower,  this has  led  to many  not having  their  cases had  for  years.  However,  there  is need  for 
collaborative research among scientist from various in other to produce efficient systems for legal service. The current level of over 
dependence on manual system and physical proceedings are also a major challenge.  Implementation of intelligent software and use 
of  appropriate technology  can improve  access  to  justice,  speed  up  the  work  and reduce  the  burden  on the  judicial  staff.  It  will 
improve the quality of judgment and reduce arbitrariness of human judges.
